# The client demo script
For live meetings (Zoom or in person) with non-technical clients.
**Live demo link:** https://superwrldcoin.github.io/ai-automation-service/demo.html

Golden rule: **don't explain the technology. Show the before → after and let the
"wow" do the selling.** You're not selling code — you're selling *hours of their life back*.

---

## Before the meeting (2 minutes of prep = deals closed)
- Open the demo link in a browser tab, ready to go.
- **If you got even a rough sample of THEIR data or paperwork beforehand:** paste it in
  first so the demo produces *their* documents. Nothing closes faster than someone
  seeing their own client names come out finished. (You can ask for it when booking:
  "Send me a sample spreadsheet and one of your templates and I'll tailor the demo.")
- Have `index.html` (the storefront) as a backup tab.

---

## The 5-minute flow (talk track)

**1. Frame the pain (30 sec) — make them nod before you show anything.**
> "Quick question before I show you anything — how much time does your team spend each
>  week copying names, prices, and details into the same documents over and over?
>  Letters, contracts, invoices, quotes — that kind of thing."

Let them answer. Whatever number they say, that's your value. Repeat it back:
> "So that's basically [X hours] a week doing the same task by hand. Let me show you
>  what I'd take off your plate."

**2. Show the two ingredients (45 sec) — keep it dead simple.**
Share your screen on the demo page. Point to the two boxes:
> "It's just two things. On the left is your information — this is just a spreadsheet,
>  like an Excel export. Each row is one client, one deal, one invoice. On the right is
>  your document — your letter or contract, written once, with blanks where the details go."

Don't say "template variables" or "CSV." Say **"spreadsheet"** and **"your document with blanks."**

**3. The magic moment (15 sec) — this is the whole demo.**
> "Now watch what happens when I click this one button."

Click **⚡ Generate documents.** Let the silence sit for a second while the documents appear.
> "That just made [N] finished, personalized documents. Instantly. Every name, every
>  price, every date — filled in and formatted correctly. No typing, no mistakes."

**4. Prove it's real (60 sec) — click into one.**
Click a document card to open the preview.
> "Here's a real one. Notice the price is formatted, the date is spelled out properly,
>  and see this highlighted section? That only appears when there's something to note —
>  the tool is smart enough to include it only when it applies."

Open a second one that's different (e.g. one without the highlighted box):
> "Different client, different details, and this part correctly *didn't* show up. Now
>  imagine this across 200 records instead of 4."

Click **Print / Save as PDF:**
> "And when you're happy, one click turns it into a PDF ready to send or sign."

**5. Make it theirs (30 sec) — the close-opener.**
> "This is a sample I built. The real version I'd make for you uses *your* documents and
>  *your* spreadsheet, so the output looks exactly like what your business sends today —
>  just produced in seconds instead of hours."

---

## Discovery → the real sale
The document demo is the hook. Now find *their* specific pain:
> "Documents are one example. What's the most repetitive, mind-numbing task in your
>  week — the thing you keep thinking 'there has to be a better way'?"

Whatever they name (data entry, moving info between apps, chasing follow-ups, monthly
reports) — that's the custom build. Say:
> "That's exactly the kind of thing I automate. Let me build you a free working sample
>  on your real data so you can see it before spending a dollar."

**Free sample = the strongest close there is.** It removes all risk for them and lets me
(Claude) build the actual proof. Bring me whatever they give you and I'll build it.

---

## Closing lines (pick by tier)
- **Entry:** "I can take that first task off your plate this week for a flat $[75–150].
  You'll have a working tool and a short video showing how to use it."
- **Core:** "Give me 15 minutes to scope it and I'll send a fixed price — no hourly, no
  surprises. Most builds land between $[400–1500] depending on the pieces."
- **Retainer:** "Once it's running, I keep it maintained and add new automations as you
  grow, for $[150–500]/month."

Then: **"Want me to start with the free sample?"** (Always end on a yes/no they can say yes to.)

---

## Objection handling
| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| "We already have someone / software for that." | "Perfect — then you know the pain. This complements it; I automate the gaps that still eat time. Let me show you on one task, free." |
| "I could just use ChatGPT." | "Great for one-offs. But you'd re-do it every time. I build the *repeatable* tool so it's one click forever. Big difference at volume." |
| "How do I know it'll work?" | "You don't have to guess — I build a working sample on your real data first, free. You only pay if it does what you need." |
| "It's not the right time." | "No problem. Can I build you one free sample anyway? If it's useful, you'll know who to call." |
| "Too expensive." | "Let's start with the $[X] entry version to prove the value, then scale up only if it's worth it to you." |

Send within an hour:
> "Great talking today. Here's the demo to play with yourself: [demo link].
>  Send me [their spreadsheet + one document] and I'll build a free sample with your
>  real data — you'll have it in [X days]."

Then hand me (Claude) their files and I'll build the actual deliverable.
