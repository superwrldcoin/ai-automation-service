# The client demo script (home-service edition)
For live meetings (Zoom or in person) with contractors — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing.

**Live tools:**
- Quote builder: https://superwrldcoin.github.io/ai-automation-service/demo.html
- Follow-up tracker: https://superwrldcoin.github.io/ai-automation-service/tracker.html
- Why-us / ROI page: https://superwrldcoin.github.io/ai-automation-service/why-us.html

Golden rule: **don't explain the technology. Show before → after and let the "wow" sell.**
You're not selling software — you're selling *hours back* and *money they're currently losing.*

---

## Before the meeting (2 min of prep = deals closed)
- Open the quote builder in one tab, the tracker in another.
- Set the **Trade** dropdown to their trade, and put **their real shop city** in the shop field.
- If they sent a sample job or price list ahead of time, have it ready to drop into the
  "Auto-build from a file" box — nothing lands harder than watching *their* quote build itself.

---

## Part 1 — The quote builder (5 min)

**1. Frame the pain (30 sec).**
> "Before I show you anything — when a customer calls, how long does it take your team to
>  get them a clean, professional quote? And be honest: how many verbal 'I'll get you a price'
>  quotes never actually get sent or followed up?"

Let them answer. Whatever they say is your opening.

**2. Show the two-second version (60 sec).** On the quote builder:
> "Watch this. Customer calls needing an AC tune-up, a new thermostat, and duct cleaning."

Check those three services. Point at the running total.
> "Prices are your prices — fully editable, it's your pricebook. Now the address."

Type the customer's address, click **Look up address**.
> "It just verified the address and — see this — added a trip charge based on the real distance
>  from your shop. No more eyeballing travel time."

**3. The magic moment (30 sec).** Click **Generate quote.**
> "That's a finished, branded quote. Deposit terms, tax, everything — in about thirty seconds
>  instead of twenty minutes at the kitchen table tonight."

Flip the **Estimate → Invoice** toggle:
> "Job's done? One click turns the same quote into an invoice. Nothing re-typed, nothing forgotten."

Click **Print / Save as PDF:**
> "And it's ready to text or email them before you've left the driveway."

**4. The 'wow' — auto-build from a description (60 sec).** Scroll to the top add-on box.
> "Here's the part people love. Say your office gets this by text or email…"

Paste something like: *"Customer needs a new water heater and a leak fixed under the sink at 1012 NE 1st Ave."* Click **Analyze & pre-fill quote.**
> "It read that, matched it to your pricebook, and pre-filled the quote — even grabbed the address.
>  Upload a spreadsheet or a PDF scope and it does the same thing. In the version I build for you,
>  this uses AI to read *any* document — inspection reports, supplier lists, emails — into a full quote."

---

## Part 2 — The follow-up tracker (3 min) — this is where the money is

Switch to the tracker tab. Click **Load sample data.**
> "Remember those verbal quotes that vanish? The industry number is about 20% of revenue lost
>  that way. Look at the top — this tells you exactly how much money is sitting in quotes that
>  need a follow-up *today*, and who to call."

Point at the red "Awaiting follow-up (at risk)" tile and the overdue rows.
> "This isn't a report you read once a month. It's a daily to-do list that pays for itself the
>  first time it saves one job you would've forgotten. Click 'Won' when you close one, and it
>  updates. And it all lives on your computer — your customer list never goes to some cloud."

## Discovery → the real sale
> "Quotes and follow-ups are the obvious win. What's the *other* thing that eats your week —
>  scheduling, chasing payments, permit paperwork, review requests?"

Whatever they name is your custom build. Then:
> "Let me build you a free working sample on your real pricebook and a couple of your real jobs,
>  so you can see it before spending a dollar."

**Free sample on their data = the strongest close there is.** Bring me (their builder) whatever
they give you — pricebook, a job sheet, their logo — and I'll build the real thing.

---

## Closing lines
- **Entry ($75–150):** "I'll set up the quote builder with your pricebook and branding this week."
- **Core ($400–1500):** "The full package — quote builder + invoicing + the follow-up tracker,
  built around your business. Fixed price after a 15-minute scoping call, no surprises."
- **Retainer ($150–500/mo):** "I keep it running, add the AI document-reading, and build new
  automations as you grow."

Always end on: **"Want me to start with the free sample?"**

## Objection handling
| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| "I already use ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro." | "Great — then you know the value. Those are big monthly subscriptions with per-job fees. What I build, you *own* — one time, no monthly bill, your data stays yours. I fit the gaps they charge extra for." |
| "I could just use ChatGPT." | "Perfect for one-offs. But you'd redo it every time. I build the repeatable tool wired to *your* pricebook, so it's one click forever." |
| "How do I know it works?" | "You don't have to guess — I build a working sample on your real pricebook first, free." |
| "Too expensive." | "Start with the $[X] entry version to prove it, then scale up only if it earns its keep. The follow-up tracker alone tends to pay for the whole thing." |
